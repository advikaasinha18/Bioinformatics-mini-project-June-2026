# Breast Cancer Bioinformatics Analysis

> An integrative bioinformatics study exploring breast cancer-associated genes, molecular pathways, gene expression, protein interactions, and prognostic significance using publicly available biological databases.

## Overview

Breast cancer is one of the most prevalent cancers worldwide and involves complex molecular mechanisms regulating cell growth, DNA repair, and apoptosis. This project investigates major breast cancer-associated genes through multiple bioinformatics approaches to understand their biological functions, interactions, expression patterns, and clinical relevance.

## Objectives

- Identify major genes associated with breast cancer
- Study their biological functions and signaling pathways
- Analyze protein-protein interaction networks
- Compare gene expression between tumor and normal tissues
- Evaluate prognostic significance using survival analysis
- Interpret the molecular mechanisms involved in breast cancer progression

## Key Findings

- Breast cancer is a multi-pathway disease.
- DNA repair genes maintain genomic stability.
- PI3K-AKT signaling plays a central role in tumor progression.
- TP53, ERBB2, and ESR1 showed significant biological and clinical relevance.
